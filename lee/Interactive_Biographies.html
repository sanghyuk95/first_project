1961

Relocation to the Place Vauban

In 1961, Pierre Bergé and Yves Saint Laurent moved to an apartment at 3 place Vauban, which reflected the tastes of two men of their time. Their home was decorated with a Knoll table, Mies Van der Rohe’s Barcelona chairs, works by Bernard Buffet, and, of course, the Senufo bird.

1961

A Logo Designed by Cassandre

In 1961, Yves Saint Laurent and Pierre Bergé asked the graphic designer Cassandre to design the house’s logo.

Born to French parents in Ukraine, Adolphe Jean Marie Mouron, who adopted the pseudonym Cassandre, was one of the world’s greatest graphic designers during the interwar period. He was appreciated for his synthetic, geometrical style and was sought after by major names, such as Christian Dior.

For Yves Saint Laurent, he created a monogram of three simple intertwined initials, which was elegant and modern just like the young man.

1963

Anne-Marie Muñoz Begins Working in the Studio

Yves Saint Laurent met Anne-Marie Muñoz at Christian Dior, where she was working when he was hired. They briefly lost contact after Saint Laurent left Dior. Muñoz spent a year working at Guy Laroche before joining Saint Laurent in 1963 to manage the studio. She would maintain this position until the haute couture house closed.

1970-1979

1970-1979

Interactive Biographies

1970

The First LOVE cards

In 1970, Yves Saint Laurent designed the first in a series of greeting cards in poster form that he would send his friends, collaborators, and clients annually until 2007.

Every year, Saint Laurent adopted a new color palette and created an image using collage, drawings, and gouache. The leitmotif of every card was the word “LOVE.” Many of the cards were inspired by Morocco, where Saint Laurent frequently created them, but also by Georges Braque, Jean Cocteau, Andy Warhol, and Henri Matisse.

1971

The Scandal Collection

On January 29, 1971, Yves Saint Laurent presented his “Libération” or “Quarante” collection inspired by 1940s wartime fashion. The couturier was inspired by Paloma Picasso, who dressed in fleamarket finds.

The short dresses, platform shoes, padded shoulders, and heavy makeup evoking Paris during the Occupation caused a scandal. The collection, which was severely criticized by the press, gave full sway to the retro trend that quickly ended up conquering popular fashion.

1971

Costumes for the Bal Proust

On December 2, 1971, Marie-Hélène de Rothschild organized a costume ball at the Château de Ferrières to celebrate the centenary of Marcel Proust’s birth. Yves Saint Laurent created costumes inspired by the Belle Époque for Nan Kempner, Hélène Rochas, Jane Birkin, and Marie-Hélène de Rothschild herself.

1971

Stage Costumes for Johnny Hallyday

After designing stage costumes for Sylvie Vartan in 1970, Yves Saint Laurent created costumes for her companion, the singer Johnny Hallyday. His designs for Hallyday’s concert at the Palais des Sports were particularly spectacular.

1980

Acquisition of the Villa Oasis and the Jardin Majorelle

In 1974, Pierre Bergé and Yves Saint Laurent acquired Dar Es Saada. In 1980, they learned that the nearby Jardin Majorelle was threatened by a real-estate development project and decided to acquire the garden and adjoining villa, which had belonged to the painter Jacques Majorelle. Together, they restored the garden and created a museum of Islamic art designed by Bill Willis in Majorelle’s former studio. In 2011, the museum was converted into a museum of Berber art.

1981 - 1982

Inspired by Matisse and Léger

Throughout his Autumn-Winter 1981 collection, Yves Saint Laurent was inspired by the artists he loved—especially Henri Matisse and Fernand Léger, whose patterns and colors deeply influenced him.

1983

Yves Saint Laurent, Proper Noun

In 1983, the couturier earned an entry in the Larousse French dictionary.

Saint Laurent (Yves), Oran, 1936, French couturier. He made his mark with his original interpretations of everyday garments (pea coat, pantsuit, etc.,), his rigorous style, and his talented use of color.

1984

Yves Saint Laurent Awarded

Yves Saint Laurent was made Chevalier de la Légion d’Honneur by François Mitterrand on March 12, 1985. He was later made Officier and then Commandeur before being named Grand Officier de la Légion d’Honneur by President Nicolas Sarkozy in 2007.

2000-2017

2000-2017

Interactive Biographies

2000

Acquisition of the Maison Prunier

In 2000, Pierre Bergé bought the restaurant Prunier. This Art Deco treasure, located on the Avenue Victor Hugo, was renowned for its seafood and the quality of its caviar. Bergé once again asked Jacques Grange to design the interior. He also acquired a fish farm in the Dordogne region, allowing the house to produce 100 % of its caviar. In 2004, Prunier joined Caviar House, becoming the Groupe Caviar House & Prunier.

2002

Creation of Pierre Bergé & Associés

In 2002, Pierre Bergé opened Pierre Bergé & Associés, his own auction house in Paris. All kinds of art objects are sold there, including paintings, orfevrerie, and wines. It was so successeful that it opened a branch in Brussels in 2006. In 2016, Pierre Bergé & Associés ranked fourth among the top ten auction houses.

2010

Opening of the Maison Cocteau

In 2002, Pierre Bergé funded the purchase of Jean Cocteau’s property in Milly-la-Forêt, which had lain abandoned for many years. He also funded the transformation of the site into a place commemorating Cocteau. It was Bergé’s way of honoring the man over whose work he has moral rights. The house underwent an extensive renovation before it opened its doors to the public in summer 2010.

2010

President of Le Monde’s Supervisory Board

At the end of the 2000s, the Groupe Le Monde was in serious debt and had to establish a recapitalization plan. Pierre Bergé joined Xavier Niel, founder of the French telecom company Free, and the investment banker Matthieu Pigasse in becoming majority shareholders in the group. He was named president of the supervisory board in December 2010.

2010

Acquisition of the Librairie des Colonnes

The Librairie des Colonnes in Tangier has been a symbolic place for literature in Morocco since 1949. Established by the Gerofi family, it was frequented by many great authors, including Samuel Beckett, Jean Genet, Juan Goytisolo, Tennessee Williams, and Truman Capote. The bookstore, which is also now a publishing house, continues to provide a window on literature and the world. The Librairie des Colonnes, which is currently managed by the writer Simon-Pierre Hamelin, was rescued by Pierre Bergé in 2009. Bergé, who loves Tangier and is passionate about literature, believes in the bookstore’s humanist project.

8 septembre 2017

Death of Pierre Bergé

On September 8, 2017, Pierre Bergé passed away in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence at the age of 86.

Pierre Bergé was a man who spent his time devoted to making beauty and excellence emerge wherever possible. A friend to artists, he was the hidden force providing them with the confidence and support they needed to create ... With the passing of Pierre Bergé, a whole portion of our literary and artistic memory has vanished. It is up to his friends and those he guided to keep this memory alive, to convey to the French people his important place in French culture, and to continue his work.

Communiqué from the President of France, Emmanuel Macron, September 8, 2017
